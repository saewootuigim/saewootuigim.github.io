About Me

I like traveling to nature and spending nights in the middle of nowhere. I try to keep adding the visited national parks in the US in my list at least once a year, but it sometimes works and sometimes doesn't.
I used to enjoy running and participated in local marathon events, several fulls, halves, and numerous 10Ks. That has been ceased for last a few years, but I am attempting to resume. Soon. When this winter ends...
I earned a Ph.D., and the dissertation was about focusing wave energy to a user-chosen subsurface region by inducing artificial seismic activity, well, of course on the ground. If we have access physically to the subsurface target, why should we have to go through such indirect way to reach there?
To wrap this differently, I solved elastic wave equations (or to be more general differential equations) in the time domain using finite element method. That was written in C and Fortran, and with HDF5, PETSc, MKL, Super LU, and etc. I also used MATLAB and Paraview for post-processing and visualization.
Now I am learning and using machine learning techniques and applying it to structural engineering research, such as predicting long-term deflection of new civil engineering material, predicting shear strength of reinforced concrete beams, categorizing anticipated failure of structural element by photo recognition, and others too. I use Python, Pandas and other libraries too.
